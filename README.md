# Spec-Driven Product Development: The Relay Scenario

## About Relay

Relay is a fictional Series B project management SaaS company. Here's what you need to know:

- **Stage:** Series B, ~200 employees, $30M ARR
- **Product:** A project management platform used by mid-market teams (50-500 people)
- **Your role:** Product Manager on the Core Platform team
- **The ask:** Your CEO wants to add an AI-powered "Smart Dependencies" feature that auto-detects task relationships between projects. Your job is to take this from vague executive request to shippable spec, through a full development cycle, and ultimately lead the rollout of spec-driven development across Relay's 4 product teams.

## How to Use This Repo

1. **Fork this repo** to your own GitHub account
2. **Work through the exercises** in order, starting with your course level
3. **Commit your work as you go** -- your completed templates and exercises become your portfolio
4. **Use the rubrics** at each capstone to self-assess before moving on

You don't need to complete a lower level before starting a higher one, but the scenario builds on itself. Level 2 assumes you have a completed spec from Level 1, and Level 3 assumes you've been through a full cycle in Level 2.

## Course Levels

### Level 1: Write Specs That Ship (Modules 1.0-1.6)

You'll take the CEO's vague "Smart Dependencies" request and turn it into a complete, shippable product spec. Along the way you'll learn to write falsifiable problem statements, run AI-assisted discovery, structure requirements using MoSCoW, and validate your spec with stakeholders.

**Capstone:** A complete product spec for the Smart Dependencies feature, ready for engineering handoff.

### Level 2: Run a Spec-Driven Cycle (Modules 2.1-2.6)

You'll take your completed spec through a full build cycle -- from sprint planning and engineering handoff through mid-cycle iteration, stakeholder management, and delivery validation. You'll learn how specs behave under pressure and how to keep them useful as reality shifts.

**Capstone:** A complete cycle retrospective covering plan, handoff, iteration, communication, and outcome assessment.

### Level 3: Lead the Transformation (Modules 3.1-3.6)

You've proven spec-driven development works on your team. Now the VP of Product wants you to roll it out across Relay's 4 product teams (Core Platform, Integrations, Mobile, and Enterprise). You'll build the executive case, design tooling strategy, lead adoption, measure results, and create a sustainability plan.

**Capstone:** A comprehensive transformation plan ready for VP-level review.

## Tips for Success

- **Don't skip the BYOP exercises.** The "Bring Your Own Product" stretches are where you apply Relay lessons to your real work. That's where lasting skill-building happens.
- **Commit early and often.** Your git history tells the story of your learning.
- **Use the rubrics honestly.** Scoring yourself a 3 and knowing why is more valuable than pretending you're a 5.
- **Revisit earlier work.** After Level 2, go back and look at your Level 1 spec. You'll see it differently.
