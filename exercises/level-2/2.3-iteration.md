# Exercise 2.3: Mid-Cycle Iteration

## Scenario Context

You're mid-way through Sprint 2. Things have mostly gone well, but reality is diverging from the plan in three ways:

**Problem 1: Precision dropped.** David tested the model on a broader dataset (not just the 3-month sample from the POC). Precision dropped from 83% to 71%. Users are getting too many false positives in the dev environment. David says he can get it back to 78% with another week of tuning, but not to 83%.

**Problem 2: The permissions edge case is bigger than expected.** Ravi discovered that 30% of enterprise accounts have project-level access controls. Showing a dependency that references a project the user can't access raises real privacy concerns. Solving this properly adds 4-5 days of work that wasn't in the sprint plan.

**Problem 3: A Should Have is getting demand.** During internal dogfooding, 3 engineers independently asked for notification when a confirmed dependency's upstream task changes status. Sophie says she could build a basic version in 2 days. But that's 2 days she doesn't have in the current sprint.

You need to decide: what changes, what stays, and how do you update the spec to reflect reality?

---

## Template Exercise

**Deliverable:** A spec amendment and revised sprint plan that respond to the three problems above while preserving the core value of the feature.

### Steps

1. **Create your iteration document** at `my-work/level-2/smart-dependencies-iteration.md`.

2. **Triage each problem.** For each of the three problems, decide:
   - Is this a blocker, a scope change, or an enhancement?
   - Does it affect a Must Have, Should Have, or Won't Have?
   - What are the options? (List at least 2 options per problem.)
   - What do you recommend and why?

   Write this up as a structured decision log.

3. **Problem 1 (Precision drop) -- Make your call.**
   Your spec says "minimum precision of 80%." The model can reach 78% with a week of tuning. Options might include:
   - Lower the threshold to 75% and add a "low confidence" indicator
   - Keep the 80% requirement and push the launch
   - Ship with 78% but add aggressive filtering for low-confidence suggestions
   - Something else

   Write your recommendation with rationale. Update the relevant requirement in a spec amendment.

4. **Problem 2 (Permissions edge case) -- Make your call.**
   This is 4-5 days of unplanned work that protects enterprise customers' data privacy. Options might include:
   - Do the work and push Sprint 3 out by a week
   - Scope down: show "dependency detected" without revealing the private project's details
   - Gate the feature: only enable cross-project detection for projects the user has access to (simpler but less valuable)
   - Something else

   Write your recommendation. Update the constraint and requirement sections of the spec.

5. **Problem 3 (Popular Should Have) -- Make your call.**
   The team wants status change notifications. It's 2 days of work. Options might include:
   - Add it to Sprint 3 if there's room after the permissions work
   - Promote it to Must Have for a fast follow release
   - Say no -- stick to the plan
   - Something else

   Write your recommendation.

6. **Write the spec amendment.** Don't rewrite the whole spec. Instead, write a short amendment document that includes:
   - **What changed:** List each requirement or constraint that changed
   - **Why it changed:** Reference the specific problem that caused the change
   - **Impact on success criteria:** Do any targets need to adjust?
   - **Impact on timeline:** Does the plan shift?

7. **Revise the sprint plan.** Update Sprint 3 based on your decisions. What work items changed? What got added, removed, or moved?

8. **Commit your iteration work:**
   ```
   git add my-work/level-2/
   git commit -m "Mid-cycle iteration: spec amendment and revised plan for Sprint 3"
   ```

### Self-Check

- Did you make decisions, or did you defer everything? A PM who can't decide in the middle of a sprint creates chaos.
- Are your decisions consistent with the spec's core intent? (If the feature stops solving the problem statement, you've iterated too far.)
- Is your spec amendment traceable -- can someone read it and understand exactly what changed and why?
- Does the revised Sprint 3 plan still produce a shippable feature?
