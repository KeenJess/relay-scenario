# Exercise 2.4: Stakeholder Management

## Scenario Context

It's the end of Sprint 2. You're about to enter Sprint 3 with a revised plan. But you have three stakeholder conversations to navigate:

**Stakeholder 1: The CEO**
The CEO pings you on Slack: "How's Smart Dependencies going? I told the board we'd have something to demo next month. Are we still on track?" The truth: you're mostly on track, but the precision drop and permissions work mean the demo won't show 83% precision as originally discussed. It'll show 78% with a confidence indicator. The CEO doesn't know the difference between precision numbers, but the board member who's a former CTO does.

**Stakeholder 2: The VP of Sales**
The VP of Sales heard about Smart Dependencies from the CEO and now wants it in the next sales deck. She sends you this email: "Can you send me 3 bullet points about Smart Dependencies for the enterprise pitch deck? We have 2 big deals in the pipeline and this could tip the scale." The feature isn't shipped yet. Promising specific functionality to enterprise prospects before it's live is risky.

**Stakeholder 3: The Integrations Team PM**
Another PM, Carlos, stops by your desk: "Hey, I heard you're building AI dependency detection. My team's been thinking about the same thing for our Jira integration -- detecting dependencies between Relay tasks and Jira tickets. Can we coordinate? Maybe share the model?" This is interesting but out of scope for your current work, and saying yes could create coupling that slows both teams down.

## Template Exercise

**Deliverable:** Three written stakeholder communications and a reflection on how the spec enabled (or failed to enable) each conversation.

### Steps

1. **Create your stakeholder document** at `my-work/level-2/smart-dependencies-stakeholder-comms.md`.

2. **Write your response to the CEO.** Draft a Slack message (keep it concise -- CEOs don't read walls of text). Your message should:
   - Confirm you're on track for the demo timeline
   - Be honest about the precision change without creating alarm
   - Frame the confidence indicator as a feature, not a compromise
   - Give the CEO enough information to brief the board accurately

   After writing the message, add a note: "What the spec enabled here." How did having a written spec (with explicit success criteria and constraints) make this conversation easier than it would have been without one?

3. **Write your response to the VP of Sales.** Draft an email. Your response should:
   - Appreciate the enthusiasm without making promises about unshipped features
   - Provide language that's accurate about what's coming without committing to specifics
   - Suggest a process (e.g., "I'll share the spec with you so you can see exactly what's landing and when")
   - Set a boundary about not including Smart Dependencies in the pitch deck until it's in beta at minimum

   After writing the email, add a note: "What the spec enabled here." How does having a spec help you manage the gap between "what Sales wants to promise" and "what's actually being built"?

4. **Write your response to Carlos.** Draft a quick in-person follow-up message (Slack or email). Your response should:
   - Show genuine interest in the integration opportunity
   - Explain why coupling the efforts now would be risky (use your spec's scope boundaries)
   - Propose a concrete next step that preserves the opportunity without creating Sprint 3 scope creep
   - Reference the Won't Have section of your spec if applicable

   After writing, add a note: "What the spec enabled here."

5. **Write a stakeholder map.** Based on this exercise, create a simple stakeholder map for Smart Dependencies:

   | Stakeholder | Interest Level | Influence | What They Need | Communication Cadence |
   |-------------|---------------|-----------|---------------|----------------------|
   | CEO | [High/Med/Low] | [High/Med/Low] | [What do they care about?] | [How often do they need updates?] |
   | VP Sales | ... | ... | ... | ... |
   | Carlos (Integrations PM) | ... | ... | ... | ... |
   | Maya (Eng Lead) | ... | ... | ... | ... |
   | Priya (CSM) | ... | ... | ... | ... |

6. **Commit your stakeholder communications:**
   ```
   git add my-work/level-2/smart-dependencies-stakeholder-comms.md
   git commit -m "Stakeholder communications: CEO, VP Sales, Integrations PM"
   ```

### Self-Check

- Is your CEO message honest without being alarming? Could the CEO use it to brief the board?
- Does your Sales response set a clear boundary without being adversarial?
- Does your response to Carlos preserve the relationship while protecting your sprint?
- In your "what the spec enabled" notes, are you being specific? (Generic answers like "the spec gave us clarity" don't count.)
