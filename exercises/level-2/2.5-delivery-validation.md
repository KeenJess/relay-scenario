# Exercise 2.5: Delivery Validation

## Scenario Context

Smart Dependencies shipped. It's been live for 30 days. Here's what you know:

**Adoption data:**
- 34% of multi-project teams have activated Smart Dependencies (your target was 40%)
- Of those who activated, 72% have confirmed or dismissed at least one detected dependency
- Average time from feature activation to first interaction: 6 hours

**Outcome data:**
- Cross-project blocked tasks have decreased by 18% across activated accounts (your target was 25%)
- The model is running at 77% precision in production (your amended target was 78%, your original was 80%)
- 89 dependencies have been confirmed by users, 147 have been dismissed
- Average user session length on the dependency view: 3.2 minutes

**Qualitative feedback:**
- Priya reports: "Enterprise customers love the concept but 3 accounts have complained about false positives. One asked if they could adjust the confidence threshold themselves."
- Sophie shares: "The dismiss button gets hit a lot. I think users are dismissing dependencies they're not sure about rather than confirming them -- the 'confirm' action feels too permanent."
- David notes: "The confirmed/dismissed feedback loop is working. Model precision should improve to ~82% in the next retrain cycle based on the signal we're getting."

The feature didn't hit every target, but it's showing real signal. Your job is to assess honestly.

## Template Exercise

**Deliverable:** A completed outcome assessment for Smart Dependencies, using `outcome-assessment-template.md`.

**Template:** [specs/templates/outcome-assessment-template.md](../../specs/templates/outcome-assessment-template.md)

### Steps

1. **Copy the template** to `my-work/level-2/smart-dependencies-outcome-assessment.md`.

2. **Score the Spec Fidelity.** Rate how well the shipped feature matched the spec (1-5). Be specific in your rationale: what stayed, what changed (precision, permissions approach), and why. Reference the spec amendment from Exercise 2.3.

3. **Write "What the Spec Got Right."** Pull at least 3 specific examples of where the spec prevented problems or accelerated decisions. Think about:
   - Did the Won't Have section prevent scope creep?
   - Did the success criteria give the team a clear target?
   - Did the constraints section keep the project technically grounded?
   - Did the edge case work from discovery prevent a nasty surprise?

4. **Write "What to Write Differently."** Pull at least 3 specific lessons. Be concrete:
   - Was the 40% activation target realistic? What would you set instead?
   - Did the UX/Design Intent section guide the team well enough on the confirm/dismiss interaction?
   - Were there requirements that turned out to be unnecessary?
   - Were there gaps -- things you should have specified but didn't?

5. **Complete the Customer Outcome table.** Use the data above to fill in actual results against each success criterion from your spec. Be honest about "Met," "Partially Met," and "Not Met."

6. **Write the qualitative feedback summary.** Synthesize what Priya, Sophie, and David shared. What pattern emerges? (Hint: the dismiss-over-confirm behavior and the false positive complaints might be related.)

7. **Write "Lessons for Next Spec."** Document at least 2 process lessons, 2 content lessons, and 1 collaboration lesson. These should be actionable -- not "write better requirements" but "every interaction-heavy feature needs a confirmation/dismissal UX spec with specific copy and undo behavior."

8. **Define follow-up actions.** What happens next? Is there a Phase 2? Do success criteria need more time? Is there a spec amendment for improvements?

9. **Commit your assessment:**
   ```
   git add my-work/level-2/smart-dependencies-outcome-assessment.md
   git commit -m "Outcome assessment: 30-day post-launch review of Smart Dependencies"
   ```

### Self-Check

- Is your assessment honest, or are you grading yourself generously? The point is to learn, not to look good.
- Are your "What to Write Differently" lessons specific enough that they'd actually change how you write your next spec?
- Do your follow-up actions have clear owners and timelines?
- Would a PM who reads this assessment learn something useful for their own work?

## BYOP (Bring Your Own Product)

Write an outcome assessment for a feature you've already shipped at work.

1. Copy the outcome assessment template to `my-work/level-2/byop-outcome-assessment.md`
2. If the feature had a spec, assess its fidelity. If it didn't have a spec, assess what a spec would have caught or prevented.
3. Score the customer outcomes honestly
4. Write lessons that are specific to your team and product

**If you haven't shipped a feature recently:** Write a *pre-mortem* instead. For your BYOP feature that's still in development, imagine it's 30 days post-launch and things went partially as planned. What would you assess?
