# Exercise 3.1: Executive Storytelling

## Scenario Context

Smart Dependencies was a hit. The feature shipped, adoption is growing, and your cycle retrospective got attention at the team offsite. Now the VP of Product, Lena, pulls you aside:

> "The other PMs were impressed by your retro presentation. Half of them admitted they've never written a proper spec. The Integrations team ships based on Slack threads and verbal commitments. The Mobile team uses PRDs that nobody reads after the kickoff. Enterprise has heavyweight specs that take 3 weeks to write and are outdated by the time they're approved. I want you to lead a rollout of spec-driven development across all 4 product teams. But first, I need you to build the case. I'm presenting to the exec team next Thursday and I need a one-pager."

Lena wants a crisp executive brief that she can present in 10 minutes. She'll fight for the initiative, but she needs ammunition.

Here are the 4 product teams at Relay:

| Team | PM | Eng Team Size | Current Process | Recent Shipping Record |
|------|-----|--------------|----------------|----------------------|
| Core Platform (you) | You | 8 engineers | Spec-driven (your pilot) | Shipped Smart Dependencies on time with 1-week slip |
| Integrations | Carlos | 6 engineers | Slack threads + verbal alignment | 2 of last 4 features required major post-launch rework |
| Mobile | Kenji | 4 engineers | Lightweight PRDs, fast iteration | Ships fast but 40% of features get redesigned within 60 days |
| Enterprise | Dana | 10 engineers | Heavyweight waterfall specs | Average cycle time: 16 weeks. Specs are thorough but inflexible. |

## Template Exercise

**Deliverable:** A completed executive brief proposing the spec-driven development rollout, using `executive-brief-template.md`.

**Template:** [specs/templates/executive-brief-template.md](../../specs/templates/executive-brief-template.md)

### Steps

1. **Copy the template** to `my-work/level-3/spec-driven-executive-brief.md`.

2. **Write "The Opportunity."** Lena needs this in 2-3 sentences that an executive can absorb in 30 seconds. Lead with the business outcome (cycle time, rework reduction, shipping confidence), not the process improvement. Executives don't buy "better specs" -- they buy "faster, more predictable shipping."

3. **Build the Evidence table.** Use your Core Platform pilot data. You have:
   - Cycle time before/after (if you set targets in your spec, use those)
   - Post-launch rework before/after (reference the outcome assessment)
   - Engineering confidence before/after (reference the handoff exercise)
   - Spec fidelity scores (from your outcome assessment)

   Also include data about the other teams' current problems (Integrations' rework rate, Mobile's redesign rate, Enterprise's cycle time) to show the gap.

4. **Write "The Ask."** Be specific:
   - What exactly do you need from the exec team? (Approval, resources, timeline commitment?)
   - How much PM time does this require?
   - Is there a tooling cost?
   - When do you need the decision?

5. **Design the Timeline.** Propose a 90-day phased rollout. Think about sequencing: which team should go first? (Hint: the team most likely to succeed early creates momentum. The team most likely to resist should not be first.)

6. **Write "Risk If We Don't Act."** Frame this in terms the exec team cares about: velocity gap between teams, compounding rework costs, talent retention risk. Use specific numbers from the team data above.

7. **Review and tighten.** Executive briefs live or die on conciseness. Go through your brief and cut every sentence that doesn't earn its place. The final document should be readable in 5 minutes.

8. **Commit your executive brief:**
   ```
   git add my-work/level-3/spec-driven-executive-brief.md
   git commit -m "Executive brief: spec-driven development rollout proposal"
   ```

### Self-Check

- Could Lena read this in 5 minutes and present it in 10 without additional context from you?
- Does the Evidence section use real data (from your pilot), not just assertions?
- Is the Ask specific enough that the exec team can say yes or no?
- Does the "Risk If We Don't Act" create urgency without being alarmist?

## BYOP (Bring Your Own Product)

Draft an executive brief for a process improvement at your real organization.

1. It doesn't have to be spec-driven development -- it can be any practice change you believe would improve your product team's output
2. Use the same template: Opportunity, Evidence, Ask, Timeline, Risk
3. Challenge yourself: could you actually send this to your VP or director?
