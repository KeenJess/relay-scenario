# Exercise 3.4: Measuring Adoption

## Scenario Context

You're in Week 6 of the rollout. The peer PM stand-ups are happening, specs are being written, but you're getting mixed signals. Lena asks: "How's the rollout going?" and you realize you don't have a data-driven answer. You have anecdotes and impressions, but no dashboard.

Here's what you've observed so far:

**Core Platform:** Continuing to use specs as before. No change needed, no change measured.

**Integrations (Carlos):** Carlos wrote his first spec. It was short but clear. His second spec is in progress. His team shipped a feature last week using the first spec and had zero rework items -- a first for them. But Carlos still grumbles about "process overhead" in stand-ups.

**Mobile (Kenji):** Kenji created the micro-spec format and his team loves it. They've written 4 micro-specs in 4 weeks. But one complex feature (offline sync redesign) got a micro-spec when it probably needed a full spec. The feature is already showing signs of scope creep.

**Enterprise (Dana):** Dana wrote a spec in 5 days (down from her usual 15). Her team used it during their sprint and actually updated it mid-cycle -- a first. But the outcome assessment step hasn't stuck: they shipped a feature and didn't do a 30-day review.

## Template Exercise

**Deliverable:** An adoption metrics dashboard and analysis of where each team stands.

### Steps

1. **Create your metrics document** at `my-work/level-3/adoption-metrics-dashboard.md`.

2. **Define your leading indicators.** These tell you whether people are doing the practice. Create a table:

4. **Score each team against the leading indicators.** Using the scenario data above, fill in actual numbers (or your best estimates) for each team at Week 6.

   | Metric | Core Platform | Integrations | Mobile | Enterprise |
   |--------|--------------|-------------|--------|-----------|
   | Spec completion rate | [%] | [%] | [%] | [%] |

   | ... | ... | ... | ... | ... |

5. **Analyze the patterns.** For each team, write a 3-4 sentence assessment:
   - Are they on track, at risk, or off track?
   - What's the leading indicator that concerns you most?
   - What intervention (if any) do you recommend?

6. **Address the Kenji problem.** The micro-spec format is being used for complex features where a full spec is needed. Design a decision framework:
   - When should a team use a full spec vs. a micro-spec?
   - What criteria determine the threshold? (Size? Complexity? Risk? Number of teams involved?)
   - Write this as a simple decision tree or checklist that any PM can apply in under 2 minutes.

7. **Address the Dana problem.** Outcome assessments aren't sticking. Diagnose why and propose a solution:
   - Is the template too heavy?
   - Is there no forcing function (nobody asks for it)?
   - Would embedding it in an existing ritual (sprint retro, quarterly review) help?

8. **Create a "traffic light" summary** for Lena -- the kind of one-glance status she can reference in exec meetings:

   | Integrations | [Status] | [Win] | [Risk] |
   | Mobile | [Status] | [Win] | [Risk] |
   | Enterprise | [Status] | [Win] | [Risk] |

9. **Commit your dashboard:**
   ```
   git add my-work/level-3/adoption-metrics-dashboard.md
   git commit -m "Adoption metrics dashboard: Week 6 status across all teams"
   ```

### Self-Check

- Are your leading indicators actually measurable, or are they aspirational? (Can you collect this data without heroic effort?)
- Do your lagging indicators connect to business outcomes the exec team cares about?
- Is your team assessment honest? (If everyone is "green," you're not looking hard enough.)
- Is the micro-spec decision framework simple enough to actually use?
