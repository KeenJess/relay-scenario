# Exercise 3.5: Sustaining the Practice

## Scenario Context

You're in Week 10 of the rollout. The initial energy is fading, which is normal. Here's what's happening:

**The good news:**
- All 4 teams have written at least 2 real specs using the standard templates
- Cross-project blocked tasks are down 22% company-wide (not just Core Platform)
- The VP of Engineering told Lena: "This is the first time I've seen all PMs speak the same language about what they're building."
- Carlos (Integrations) privately admitted the spec prevented a major misalignment on his last feature. He's still not a cheerleader, but he's no longer resisting.

**The warning signs:**
- Kenji's team skipped the spec on their last feature ("it was small, we didn't need one"). The feature shipped with a bug that a spec review would have caught.
- Dana's team produced a beautiful spec for their latest feature but fell back to their old habit of never updating it. The spec and the shipped feature diverged significantly.
- Your PM stand-up attendance dropped from 4/4 PMs to 2/4 last week.
- Nobody has done an outcome assessment since your Smart Dependencies example. The practice isn't sticking.
- A new PM, Asha, just joined the Enterprise team. She's never written a spec in this format. Who onboards her?

You need to build a sustainability plan that keeps this practice alive after your direct involvement ends.

## Template Exercise

**Deliverable:** A sustainability plan that addresses embedding the practice in existing rituals, handling new team members, and preventing backsliding.

### Steps

1. **Create your sustainability plan** at `my-work/level-3/sustainability-plan.md`.

2. **Embed specs into existing rituals.** The fastest way to kill a new practice is to require new meetings. Instead, integrate specs into things that already happen. For each existing ritual, describe how spec-driven development plugs in:

   | Existing Ritual | How Specs Plug In | Change Required |
   |----------------|-------------------|-----------------|
   | Sprint planning | [e.g., No feature enters sprint planning without an approved spec] | [What needs to change about how sprint planning currently works?] |
   | Sprint retrospective | [e.g., Add "spec fidelity check" as a standing retro question] | [Change required] |
   | Quarterly product review | [e.g., Include a "spec health" slide with adoption metrics] | [Change required] |
   | 1:1s between PM and Eng Lead | [e.g., Spec quality is a discussion topic in monthly 1:1s] | [Change required] |
   | New feature kickoff | [e.g., Spec walkthrough replaces the old "verbal brief" format] | [Change required] |
   | Post-ship retrospective | [e.g., Outcome assessment template becomes the retro format] | [Change required] |

3. **Create a "Spec Champions" program.** Each team needs someone who owns spec quality after you step back. Design this role:
   - Who qualifies? (Not just the PM -- could be a tech lead who values clear specs)
   - What do they do? (Review specs, onboard new team members, update templates, represent their team at quarterly template review)
   - How much time does it take? (Be realistic -- if it's more than 1 hour/week, it won't survive)
   - How do you recognize and reward champions? (Public recognition? Career growth? Just gratitude?)

4. **Design the new PM onboarding flow.** Asha just joined Enterprise. She needs to get up to speed on spec-driven development. Write a 1-week onboarding plan:
   - Day 1: [What does Asha read?]
   - Day 2: [What does Asha do?]
   - Day 3-4: [Practice exercise]
   - Day 5: [First review with spec champion]

5. **Address each warning sign.** Write a specific response to each:
   - **Kenji skipping specs:** What's the forcing function to prevent this? (Cultural norm? Gate in Linear? Direct conversation?)
   - **Dana's team not updating specs:** What makes mid-cycle updates the path of least resistance?
   - **Stand-up attendance dropping:** Should the stand-up continue? Change format? Change cadence? End and replace with something asynchronous?
   - **Outcome assessments not sticking:** Is the template too heavy? Should it be simplified? Embedded in an existing process?

6. **Design a "spec health" quarterly review.** Once per quarter, all PMs review the state of spec-driven development. Define:
   - What data do you review? (Adoption metrics, spec fidelity scores, cycle time trends)
   - What decisions get made? (Template updates, process tweaks, recognition of strong work)
   - Who attends? (PMs, eng leads, Lena?)
   - How long is it? (60 minutes max)

7. **Write your own exit criteria.** You can't lead this initiative forever. Define what "done" looks like for your personal involvement:
   - What conditions must be true before you hand off the transformation lead role?
   - Who takes over?
   - What ongoing support (if any) do you provide?

8. **Commit your sustainability plan:**
   ```
   git add my-work/level-3/sustainability-plan.md
   git commit -m "Sustainability plan: embedding spec-driven development for the long term"
   ```

### Self-Check

- Does every element of your plan attach to an existing ritual, or did you create new process?
- Is the Spec Champions role sustainable at 1 hour/week, or did you overload it?
- Would your new PM onboarding work for someone who has never seen a spec before?
- Are your responses to warning signs direct and actionable, or generic advice?
